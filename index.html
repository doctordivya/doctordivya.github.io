Specialist in Pulmonary medicine. Her expertise lies in Asthma and Alergy, Lung Infections, Pneumonia, COPD, Sleep disorders and any other respiratory diseases.

COPD

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Even for more advanced stages of disease, effective therapy is available that can control symptoms, reduce your risk of complications and exacerbations, and improve your ability to lead an active life.

Asthma

Asthma

The right medications for you depend on a number of things — your age, symptoms, asthma triggers and what works best to keep your asthma under control. Your treatment should be flexible and based on changes in your symptoms, which should be assessed thoroughly each time you see your doctor.

Allergy

Allergy

Seek medical care each time allergy occurs. Even if their symptoms go away. Your doctor will help you take steps to identify and avoid your allergy triggers. Depending on your allergy, medications can help reduce your immune system reaction and ease symptoms.

In the case of a medical emergency, go directly to the emergency department of your nearest hospital or call 108.

Sleep disorders

Your doctor may recommend cognitive behavioral therapy, medications or both, to help improve relaxation and sleep, also provide strategies related to your lifestyle and sleep environment to help you develop habits that promote sound sleep and daytime alertness.

Other Respiratory Problems

Your doctor can help you with Bronchitis, Emphysema, Lung Cancer, Pneumonia, Fibrosis/Bronchiectasis, etc.

Tests

Spirometry

Spirometry

Spirometry may be carried out to check the severity of the condition and to see how you're responding to treatment. It can also be used to help diagnose a lung condition if you have symptoms of a problem, or your doctor feels you're at an increased risk of developing a particular lung condition.
